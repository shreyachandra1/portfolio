About Me



Our future is technology and I, for one, wish to be dead center in it. My name is Shreya Chandra and I am a freshman at Dublin High School who aspires to be a Software Engineer. This is my career of choice because I want to improve the technology used in our daily lives and be able to have my own creative freedom to develop products that will improve our society as a whole. My intended path to reach this career goal is to immerse myself as much as possible within engineering throughout these next four high school years. My first steps to doing so are taking Computer Science Principles, an introductory course that teaches programming and the basics behind the workings of the internet. I have joined Build Club, in which we peer tutor, build machines to assist in the Special Olympics, and spread the word of engineering around. Through this club, I am able to attend 3D printing workshop sessions along with meeting guest speakers every few weeks to learn about their story and how they have gotten to the STEM based job they are at now. In the spring of 2019, I plan to apply for the Engineering Academy to build my foundational knowledge of engineering and gather real world experience in this field of study.

Although I have not yet gained much experience within the world of Computer Science, I have had experience participating in other activities that have tested my abilities. I am a figure skater, so I strive to wake up and practice before my school day begins, five mornings a week. I have been doing this sport for nearly 8 years, along with 4 years of experience being on a synchronized skating team. I was captain for 2 of those 4 years, having learned the resilience and patience needed to keep everyone on my team accountable. I am no longer a member of the team, for I wish to pursue this passion as an individual competitor. Figure skating as a whole has taught me to keep myself accountable as I alone am responsible for how well I perform and to be disciplined, as I have had to sacrifice many experiences other peers my age get to enjoy in lieu of practices or competitions.

Over the summer of 2018, I worked as a camp counselor at Dublin Iceland to provide children with fairly little to no background experience skating the chance to learn the basics of this sport. I needed to keep track of my own group of campers, supervising them during practice time, choreographing a routine for them to perform at the end of the week, and ensuring positive camper experience through group bonding activities. This experience has taught me to remain patient and calm when working with others, for things would not always work out the way I wanted them to. I used the first few days on the job to mirror other counselors and understand how the camp dynamic worked, which I quickly picked up on. I was unafraid to ask my head supervisor questions and worked cooperatively with my fellow counselors, unafraid to handle some of the more dreaded tasks. I have never been one to back down from challenging concepts/tasks and I will always be a dedicated team member. Thank you for taking the time to read through my portfolio and consider me to be part of your workforce. I promise I will not disappoint!
