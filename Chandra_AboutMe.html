Online Portfolio

Shreya Chandra

Our future is technology and I, for one, wish to be dead center in it. My name is Shreya Chandra and I am a freshman at Dublin High School who aspires to become a Software Engineer. This is my career of choice because I want to improve the technology used in our daily lives and be able to have my own creative freedom to develop products that will improve the daily lives of everyone. My intended path to reach this career goal is to immerse myself as much as possible within engineering throughout these next four years. My first steps to doing so are taking Computer Science Principles, an introductory course that teaches me programming and the basics behind the workings of the internet. I have joined Build Club, in which we spread the word of engineering around as much as possible, attending 3D printing workshop sessions along with meeting guest speakers every few weeks to learn about their story and how they have gotten to the STEM based job they are at now. In the spring of 2019, I plan to apply for the Engineering Academy to truly test and enrich my skills.

Although I have not yet gained much experience within the world of Computer Science, I have had experience participating in other activities that have tested my abilities and I continue to push myself. I am a figure skater, so I strive to wake up and practice before my school day begins, five mornings a week. I have been doing this sport for 7.5 years now, along with four years of experience being on a synchronized skating team. I was captain for 2.5 of those 4 years, having learned the resilience and patience needed to keep everyone on my team accountable. I am no longer a member of the team, for I wish to pursue this passion as an individual competitor. Figure skating as a whole has taught me to keep myself accountable as I alone am responsible for how well I perform and to be disciplined, as I have had to sacrifice many experiences other peers my age get to enjoy in lieu of practice or competition.

Over the summer of 2018, I worked as a camp counselor at Dublin Iceland to provide children with fairly little to no background experience skating the chance to learn how to learn the basics of this sport. I needed to keep track of my own group of children, supervising them during practice time, choreographing a routine for them to perform at the end of the week, and taking disciplinary action when necessary to make sure everything ran as smoothly as it should have. This experience has certainly taught me to remain patient and calm when working with others, for things would not always work out the way I wanted them to. I used the first few days on the job to mirror others and understand how the camp dynamic work, which I quickly picked up on. I wasn't afraid to ask questions to my head supervisor and worked cooperatively with my fellow counselors, unafraid to handle some of the more dreaded tasks. I have never been one to back down from challenging concepts/tasks and I will always be a dedicated team member. Thank you for taking the time to read through my portfolio and consider me to be part of your workforce. I promise I will not disappoint!
