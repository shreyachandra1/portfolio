About Me



Our future is technology and I, for one, wish to be dead center in it. My name is Shreya Chandra and I am a sophomore at Dublin High School who aspires to work in the field of Computer Science and Engineering. This is my career of choice because I want to play a role in improving the technology used in our daily lives, as well as have the creative freedom to develop efficient and engaging products. My intended path to reaching this goal is to immerse myself fully in engineering throughout my high school years. I have already begun to do so by taking AP Computer Science Principles in my freshman year, an introductory college-level course that taught programming and the basics of the internet, in which I received a 5 on the AP Exam. As of Spring 2019, I have enrolled in Project Lead the Way's Engineering Academy to build my foundational knowledge of engineering and gather real world experience in this field of study. I am currently taking Honors Principles of Engineering to learn the foundations and survey various fields of engineering through hands-on activities and projects, all of which assist in building teamwork skills. I have been a member of Build Club since Fall 2018, through which I peer tutor students in Computer Science and participate in the Outreach program, in which I am able to attend 3D printing workshop sessions and meet guest speakers every few weeks to learn about their story and how they have climbed their way through the fields of STEM. Through my school's Technovation Club/Program, which encourages girls to participate in a computer science and entrepreneurship competition, this year I have also taken up a passion project of developing and marketing a climate change app meant to inform players of worldwide environmental problems and how they can take steps to alleviating these issues, all while enjoying a fun game.

Although I still have much to learn from both Engineering and Computer Science, I have had experience participating in other activities that have tested my abilities. I am a figure skater, meaning I wake up and practice before my school day begins, five mornings a week. I have been an avid competitor in this sport for 8 years. Throughout my years as an athlete, I have learned how to hold myself accountable as I alone am responsible for how well I perform, thus I have had to balance both a rigorous course schedule along with the demands of training many hours both on and off the ice. Furthermore, this sport has instillled discipline within me, as I have had to sacrifice many experiences other peers my age get to enjoy in lieu of practices or competitions, along with eating nutritious meals on a daily basis and ensuring I remain focused during practices and conditioning.

Over the summer of 2018, I worked as a camp counselor at Dublin Iceland to provide children with fairly little to no background experience skating the opportunity to learn the basics of this sport. I needed to keep track of my own group of campers, supervising them during practice time, choreographing a routine for them to perform at the end of the week, and ensuring positive camper experience through group bonding activities. This experience has taught me to remain patient and calm when working with others, for things would not always work out the way I wanted them to. I used the first few days on the job to mirror other counselors and understand how the camp dynamic worked, which I quickly picked up on. I was unafraid to ask my head supervisor questions and worked cooperatively with my fellow counselors, resolute when handling some of the more dreaded tasks. I have never been one to back down from challenging concepts/tasks and will always be a diligent team member. Thank you for taking the time to read through my About Me and considering me to be part of your workforce. I invite you to explore the rest of my website and I promise I will not disappoint!
